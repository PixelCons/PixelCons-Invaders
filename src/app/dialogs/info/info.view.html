New Invaders can be uncovered (minted) by anyone who owns PixelCons.
Algorithmic Generation
New Invaders are algorithmically generated using a corresponding PixelCon as a seed. Only PixelCons created before January 1st, 2022 can uncover new Invaders.
The older a PixelCon is, the more Invaders it is able to uncover with a max of 6. The current owner of a PixelCon is the only one who can uncover (mint) its new Invaders.
When an Invader is uncovered, it is created as a PixelCon and then wrapped over to the Invaders collection.
Only {{ctrl.maxInvaders}} Invaders can be uncovered in total. Once all Invaders from a PixelCon have been uncovered, the PixelCon can no longer uncover new Invaders.
Scanning for More
The Find More feature reports what PixelCons are currently listed for sale on OpenSea and previews the Invaders they can still uncover.
PixelCon Invaders live on a Ethereum rollup (also known as Layer 2) that enable cheaper transactions than the Ethereum Mainnet, while not compromising on security.
Uncovering Invaders to Layer 2
Since PixelCon Invaders are created as PixelCons on the Ethereum Mainnet first, they must go through a bridge process to get over to Layer 2. This process can take up to 15 minutes but once complete, you can expect cheaper and faster transactions! Read more about Optimistic Ethereum (Layer 2)
